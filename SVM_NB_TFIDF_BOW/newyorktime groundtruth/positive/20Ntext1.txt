President Bush used an attack on his honesty by Gov. Bill Clinton as an occasion to mount his sharpest assault yet on his rival's fitness for office. The President -- on the third stop of a train trip through Wisconsin intended to project an image of confidence and momentum -- then unleashed a scorching recital of what he called Mr. Clinton's deceptions and reversals on seven issues, including his draft history, his use of marijuana and his positions on free trade, right-to-work laws and the Persian Gulf war. BILL CLINTON
